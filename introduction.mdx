# Introduction

The **SigmaEpsilon Solid Fourier** library provides semi-analytic solutions for beam and plate bending problems using Navier's method. By carefully choosing approximating functions that inherently satisfy boundary conditions, the library delivers fast, accurate results for simply-supported structures.

## What is SigmaEpsilon Solid Fourier?

This library solves structural mechanics problems for:
- **Beams**: Euler-Bernoulli and Timoshenko models
- **Plates**: Kirchhoff-Love and Mindlin-Reissner models

Unlike finite element methods that discretize the entire domain, Navier's method uses Fourier series to represent the displacement field, resulting in significantly faster calculations when applicable.

</Warning>

## Key Features

- **Semi-analytic Solutions**: Fast and accurate results for beam and plate bending
- **Multiple Load Types**: Support for point loads, line loads, rectangular loads, and disk loads
- **Arbitrary Loading**: Monte-Carlo based coefficient determination for complex load distributions
- **High Performance**: Industry-grade parallel computing for rapid calculations
- **Python Integration**: Seamless integration with NumPy, SciPy, xarray, and other scientific libraries
- **Visualization Ready**: Built-in examples for Matplotlib plotting
- **Both Beam Models**: Euler-Bernoulli (without shear deformation) and Timoshenko (with shear deformation)
- **Both Plate Models**: Kirchhoff-Love (thin plates) and Mindlin-Reissner (thick plates)

## Navier's Method Overview

Navier's method solves bending problems by representing the displacement field as a double Fourier series:

<Info>
For a simply-supported rectangular plate, the deflection is approximated as:

```
w(x, y) = Σ Σ W_mn · sin(mπx/a) · sin(nπy/b)
```

where:
- `W_mn` are the unknown Fourier coefficients
- `m, n` are harmonic indices
- `a, b` are the plate dimensions
</Info>

The sine functions automatically satisfy the simply-supported boundary conditions (zero displacement and zero moment at edges). The unknown coefficients are determined by:

1. Substituting the series into the governing differential equation
2. Expanding the load as a Fourier series
3. Solving the resulting algebraic system

<Tip>
The accuracy of the solution depends on the number of harmonic terms included. More terms capture finer details but increase computation time. Typically, 50-100 terms provide excellent accuracy for most engineering applications.
</Tip>

## Theory Background

### Beam Models

**Euler-Bernoulli Beam**: Assumes plane sections remain plane and perpendicular to the neutral axis. Suitable for slender beams where shear deformation is negligible.

**Timoshenko Beam**: Accounts for shear deformation and rotational inertia. Required for thick or short beams, or when shear flexibility is significant.

### Plate Models

**Kirchhoff-Love Plate**: Classical thin plate theory assuming normals to the mid-surface remain straight and normal after deformation. Valid when thickness `t` is small compared to lateral dimensions (t/a < 1/20).

**Mindlin-Reissner Plate**: Accounts for transverse shear deformation. Necessary for thick plates or when accurate shear stress distribution is required.
